Key HR Insights from EDA

  • Attrition rate: 16.1% - high enough to impact costs and morale.
  • Overtime impact: 30.5% attrition when OverTime = Yes (vs 10.4% No) - major burnout driver.
  • Income age: Leavers have lower median monthly income - possible dissatisfaction trigger.
  • Age & tenure: Employees under 35 and with <5 years tenure show higher turnover.
  • Satsifaction: Lower Job & Environment satisfaction strongly linked to leaving.

Actionable Recommendations

  1. Reduce overtime exposure
  • Conduct overtime audits and cap weekly hours (align with BCEA limits).
  • Introduce flexible shifts or compensatory time-off for high-overtime roles.
  1. Address salary equity
  • Benchmark MonthlyIncome against market rates for role with high attrition (sales, young employees).
  • Implement targeted salary adjustment or performance-linked bonuses for at-risk groups.
  1. Support early-career employees
  • Launch mentorship programs for staff under 35 and <5 years tenure.
  • Run quartly engagemnt surveys found on young employees.
  1. Improve satisfaction & work-life balance
  • Roll out wellness initiatives (e.g EAP programs, mental health days).
  • Train managers on creating supportive environments (target low jobsatisfaction scores).
  1. Monitor & follow up
  • Track attrition monthly by Department, OverTime and Age Group.
  • Set KPI: Reduce attrition by 20% in high-risk groups within 12 months.

These targeted actions can lower turnover costs and improve employee retention.
